About the Authors



Ann Pearson

Ann Pearson currently serves as the director of Curriculum and Catalog at San Jacinto College in Pasadena, Texas, where she often also teaches composition and literature. She earned her BA in English at Texas A&M University in College Station, Texas, and her MA and PhD in English at the University of New Mexico in Albuquerque, New Mexico. Ann is a lifelong learner who enjoys history, reading, traveling, and writing. Combining those interests, she has written historic fiction about the 1900 Galveston Storm and life on the Texas frontier, with many stories yet to tell. She lives just outside Houston with her husband, Dennie.

Sabrina Mathues

Sabrina Mathues is a full-time faculty member and department chair for College Success at Brookdale Community College in Lincroft, New Jersey. She has previously served as acting dean of Academic Services and director of Off-Campus Programs at Ocean County College, coordinator of Academic Progress at Montclair State University, and undergraduate program coordinator and academic advisor for physics at Arizona State University. Her leadership roles have included terms as state liaison to NACADA (the Global Community for Academic Advising) for Arizona and New Jersey, assistant project manager for Strategic Planning at Ocean County College, and as a Guided Pathways consultant at community colleges throughout New Jersey. She holds a master of education degree in higher and postsecondary education from Arizona State University. Her areas of research interest focus on teaching methods in first-year seminar courses and career planning among undecided students. Sabrina resides near the Jersey Shore, where she enjoys collecting sea glass and acrylic painting. She would like to acknowledge her son, Noah, for being a model of boundless energy and for his immense patience.

Guy Lefrançois

Guy Lefrançois makes few claims to anything notable. “I’m an obscure French Canadian from a long line of obscure ancestors,” he insists. “Like me,” he says, “most are better remembered not for the things they did but for those they never accomplished. Some of the things I haven’t done are truly amazing!” He currently holds an appointment at the University of Alberta, where he received his PhD and where he started teaching in 1966. Since then, he has written numerous books on psychology, many of which have been translated into other languages, including Chinese, Russian, Italian, Spanish, Portuguese, and German (he does not speak all these languages). He has been happily married to Marie for more years than he can easily count and loves to fish, watch birds, pick berries, read other people’s words, and go on extended bicycle tours. He has three children (Laurier, Claire, and Rémi) and five grandchildren to whom this book is dedicated.

Preface



College & Career Success: Developing Skills & Strategies aims to guide students toward success in college and beyond. Students will learn the importance of making a plan for success, including setting goals, practicing time and energy management, and developing the right mindset to approach challenges along the way. Through self-assessment and reflection, metacognition, self-regulation, and personal learning tools, students can develop skills and strategies that will help them succeed in the online educational environment and the workplace.

Textbook Features

College & Career Success: Developing Skills & Strategies includes a number of features to help students understand key concepts:

Try It feature boxes present opportunities for students to apply concepts they have just learned.

Tech Tips feature boxes include links, apps, and other technology resources students can use in their learning journey.

Communication Skills feature boxes include guidance on how to effectively communicate in an academic and professional manner.

Figures and images throughout the book illustrate key concepts.

Interactions and videos in the ebook help students understand, explore, and apply difficult concepts.

Worksheets and templates are available for download in the ebook and allow students to complete some of the exercises described in the book and record their answers.

Key Terms list and define key concepts as discussed in each chapter. Click on any term in the ebook Key Terms section to see the definition.
